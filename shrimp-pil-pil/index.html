Shrimp Pil-Pil

29 April 2015

This recipe is my take on one of my favorite appetizers, served at a quaint little Greek restaurant that we used to live by. Since we lived across the street, we frequented Dimitris numerous times, and always ended up with this appetizer since we loved it so much. What we know : it’s spicy, it’s garlicky, and it is crispy and delicious. I think I came pretty close to recreating this recipe on the first try. Before writing this, I did a quick google search and learned that this a common tapas recipe from Spain, called Gambas al Ajillo.

Since returning from our trip to the islands of Belize, I have been craving seafood. On the islands, it is so easy because it is always the fresh catch of the day. I ate fish and seafood every night and loved it. I have been inspired to try more fish dishes and experiment with new types of fish and seafood. I hope to have some new and exciting fish dishes coming up in the near future.

  • 1 serrano chile, diced fine (can sub jalapeno)
  • ½ lb of shrimp - peeled and deveined
  • 5 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 scallion, chopped
  • ½ teaspoon red pepper flakes
  • cilantro - minced
  • ~ ¼ cup flour
  • 2 teaspoons olive oil
  • squeeze of lemon, optional

To begin, make sure shrimp are cleaned and patted until completely dry. Add salt and pepper to shrimp and then dunk them in the flour, covering all sides with a light coat. Next prepare your veg. Dice the chile, garlic, and scallion. Add oil to the pan and let it sizzle. Add in just the chile and scallion and let it cook for about 3 minutes. Add your dredged shrimp and let it cook and crisp up. It can take about 3-5 minutes per side, depending on the size of your shrimp.

After you flip the shrimp, add the garlic in. Garlic has a tendency to burn, so we want to prevent this by adding it later. Once the shrimp is almost done, toss everything together in the pan. Add in chili flakes and cilantro and let the flavors marry. Make sure you scrape up all the bits of flavor from the bottom of the pan. Serve with a squeeze of lemon, and even grilled bread to sop up the excess!

Kristina Bowser
