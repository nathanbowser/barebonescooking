Greek Panzanella Chicken

17 July 2015

Here it is : the perfect summer meal! I know I have been saying this a lot, but this truly is it. It is fresh, light, healthy, and absolutely delicious. It comes together in no time and some can be made ahead of time. We are now into the perfect time of year where the tomatoes are bursting with flavor and they really shine here. The feta cheese is salty and goes perfectly with the greek salad dressing. The bread cubes are the best part; they are crunchy and they soak in all the great juices. We devoured this for dinner, each bite better as the salad sits and gains flavor. The salad portion of this meal is so good that you could even serve it as a dinner on its own!

  • 1 cucumber - chopped
  • 1 large tomato - chopped
  • 1/2 red onion - sliced
  • ¼ cup kalamata olives - chopped
  • 1 block feta cheese - rough chop
  • baguette bread cubes - toasted
  • chicken thighs or chicken breasts

Greek Dressing (adapted from Ina Garten’s recipe on foodnetwork)

  • 4 cloves garlic, grated
  • 2 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • 1/2 cup red wine vinegar
  • 2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 cup olive oil

*Note: the dressing recipe is doubled as I listed it. If you are making the salad alone, cut it in half. I used half of the dressing for the chicken and half for the salad.

Make the dressing first. You will mix together the ingredients, then separate it equally in half. It makes a little less than 2 cups, so about ¾ cups each. Use half to marinate the chicken. I marinated mine for a few hours, but the longer the better with chicken. Set the other dressing aside for later.

Prep all of your veggies : cucumber, tomato, red onion, olives, and feta. Add them to a large bowl and pour some of the dressing over them to begin marinating.

Add your chicken to the grill or skillet and cook until finished. While chicken is cooking, cut your baguette into large cubes. Add a thin layer of olive oil to a pan and crisp up the bread slices. They will only take 3-4 minutes per side.

Pull your chicken off the heat and let it rest. Add the bread cubes to the salad and toss the remaining dressing over the bread and mix. To serve : put the chicken on the bottom of the plate and pile it high with the salad! This is the true taste of summer :)

We had the salad leftover the next day; the only thing you have to do is make new bread cubes because they will get soggy. The rest of the salad is even better the next day!

Kristina Bowser
