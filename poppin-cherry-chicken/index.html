Poppin' Cherry Chicken

24 June 2015

Here we have a perfect summer meal. Summertime is the best time for tomatoes to shine. This recipe is my spin on the classic chicken parm. The chicken is crispy and flavorful. The spinach is healthy and garlicky. The tomatoes are sweet and burst with flavor in your mouth. The cheese on top is ooey and gooey and ties everything together.

We had this for Sunday dinner for my brother and his girlfriend. It was pretty fun to have extra hands in the kitchen to help with the prep. So bring your family, friends, and kids into the kitchen and make this before summer is over!

  • 1 cup flour
  • 3 eggs
  • 2 cups bread crumbs ( I used seasoned)
  • *6 thin sliced chicken breasts
  • 1 pint cherry tomatoes
  • 8 oz fresh mozzarella
  • 1 bag spinach
  • 2 cloves of garlic - minced

*the chicken must be sliced thin - if you buy the large chicken breasts, cut them in half lengthwise

For this meal, I find it best to prep all of the parts and then assemble the chicken and bake.

For the chicken breading :
Begin by setting up your breading station. Add the flour, eggs, and breadcrumbs each into a separate container. Pat your chicken dry and add salt and pepper. Next, dip the chicken in flour, then into the egg, and finally into the breadcrumbs. Set on a late until you are ready to pan-fry. Once you are ready to cook, shallow fry each piece in enough olive oil to coat the pan. Since they are thin, they will cook quickly - 3-4 minutes per side. Once cooked, add to a paper towel to drain.

Cherry Tomatoes :
Add oil, salt, and pepper to tomatoes and roast on 350 degrees for 20 minutes. You want the tomatoes to be at the point where they just burst (see below).

Spinach :
Saute in olive oil to wilt the spinach down. After about 5-7 minutes when it is cooked, add a pinch of salt and pepper and the minced garlic.

For the Dish :
Take a piece of chicken and add the spinach on top. Next, add the cherry tomatoes and mozzarella cheese. Since everything is already cooked at this point, we just want to melt the cheese. Put your oven on the broil setting and broil the chicken on the top rack for 2-3 minutes until cheese is melted and bubbly.

I served this alongside a simple pasta with the rest of the roasted tomatoes from the prep, roasted garlic, and parmesan cheese.
