Chicken Ka-pita-bobs

29 April 2015

I know what you are thinking - Is it a kabob or a pita? Well I thought it would be fun to turn the chicken kabobs into a Greek/Lebanese style pita with tzatziki! It spices up your chicken routine and you can make it on the grill. I want to say grilling season is here for good, but who knows with this weather! Hopefully it will turn around in May. Once we start grilling outside, we grill straight through until winter hits. It is so nice to be outside grilling, plus it is less dishes!

This recipe is just a basic and easy marinade. As for the veggies, you can use what you like, I just used onions this time. The tzatziki recipe is actually not my own. I know I am Lebanese and I am positive my gram has a spectacular recipe for this, but I have been making Ina Garten’s recipe for years, and it is just too good to change. It is utterly perfect and we love it every time. I will post a link to it so you can check it out. You can top these pitas with tomatoes, onions, pickles, anything you like. They are healthy and delicious way to jump out of that grilled chicken rut!

Kabobs

  • 2 pounds of chicken breasts or tenders, cut into 1 inch cubes
  • ¼ cup olive oil
  • the juice of 1 lemon
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced or grated
  • 2 teaspoons fresh dill
  • salt and pepper
  • 1 large white onion, cut into chunks
  • skewers

Pitas

  • pitas, I used whole wheat
  • fixins : tomatoes, red onions
  • Tzatziki

I find that if I take the extra time out to marinate the chicken, it comes out juicier in the end. Chop the chicken into equal size cubes and place in a large ziplock bag or in a dish to marinate. Add in a good amount of salt and pepper, then the oil, lemon juice, garlic, and dill. Marinate for at least 3 hours but up to an entire day. Once chicken is marinated, grab your skewers and thread the chicken and the white onion (or veggies), alternating. Grill the skewers until the chicken is cooked through to 165 degrees. Let them rest while you prepare the pitas, tzatziki and toppings.

To make the pita, add a skewer of chicken meat and onions, top with tzatziki and veggies! This recipe can really be adapted to the veggies and toppings you want, so have fun with it.

Kristina Bowser
