Meatless Monday : Stuffed Peppers and Tomatoes

16 August 2015

Hello again! Sorry for my brief hiatus; vacation, work, and summer got the best of me for a little bit. However, I have a bunch of great recipes lined up and ready to go and a few more tricks up my sleeve before summer is over. These stuffed peppers and tomatoes are really great for any season, but the tomatoes are at their peak right now.

You will certainly not miss the meat in these hearty stuffed peppers and tomatoes. We like to eat meatless around here at least once a week and I find it especially easier in the summer when the veggies are abundant and fresh. Since it is the heart of summer and the tomatoes are perfect and ripe, I decided to stuff half peppers and half tomatoes. The peppers are firmer and tasty and the tomatoes are juicy and sweet. This is a great combo for a summer dinner. The quinoa and the beans add a lot of protein to the meal, so you will be full and satisfied. These will also make great leftovers, you can easily double the ingredients and make more!

Meatless Stuffed Peppers and Tomatoes

  • ¼ cup red onion - diced
  • 1 ear of cooked corn - kernels removed
  • 1/2 can black beans
  • 1 jalapeno - diced fine
  • ½ cup shredded cheese
  • ½ cup quinoa
  • ½ red bell pepper - diced
  • 1 cup enchilada sauce - split into 2 parts
  • 1 scallion - chopped
  • sour cream for serving

Begin by cooking your quinoa. The ratio here is 2:1. 1/2 cup quinoa and 1 cup of liquid (water or broth). Bring to a boil and then simmer for 15-20 minutes until all liquid is absorbed. Set aside.

Next, saute peppers and onion for about 5 minutes just to soften. Add a pinch of salt and pepper to the mix. Set aside.

In a large bowl, add quinoa, peppers/onions, corn kernels, black beans, and cheese. Mix everything together, then add ½ cup of enchilada sauce. Let the flavors mix while you prep your peppers and tomatoes.

Preheat the oven to 350 degrees.

Hollow out the peppers but removing the stem and the inside seeds and ribs. As for the tomatoes, use a small pairing knife to cut around the top, remove the stem, and scoop out the seeds and the insides. Usually when I make stuffed peppers, the thing that bothers me is that the peppers do not fully cook in the amount of time that everything else does, leaving you with a semi-crunchy pepper. Well, I have a new-found tip for solving this problem. Boil just the peppers in water for approximately 5 minutes to soften them. Then drain them and place them into the dish to stuff. Note: you do not need to do this with the tomatoes as they will cook just fine in the oven.

Once everything is ready, stuff the peppers and tomatoes to the top with the mixture. Push down to fit as much as you can. Pour the remaining ½ cup of enchilada sauce on the bottom of the dish to keep them moist. Cover everything tightly in foil and bake on 350 degrees for 10 minutes. After 10 minutes, remove from the oven, add cheese on top and bake for another 15 minutes until done. You can broil them for 1-2 minutes at the end if you want to cheese to get bubbly!

These are perfect for meatless monday - healthy and filling!

Kristina Bowser
