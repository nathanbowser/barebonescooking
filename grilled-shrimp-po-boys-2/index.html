Grilled Shrimp Po' Boys

22 August 2015

I love a good po’ boy sandwich, who doesn’t? However, especially in the summer, you don’t want a heavy deep fried sandwich, so I thought I would try and lighten things up a bit. Still all the same great po’ boy flavor, minus most of the fat! BY grilling the shrimp, you get a great flavor on them and a nice little char. This is a really quick meal that can come together in snap. The shrimp do not need any time to marinate and the sauce is just simply mixing everything together. This is great to make on a nice summer evening with a nice cold beverage. Hope you enjoy!

Shrimp

  • ½ lb peeled and deveined shrimp (the bigger, the better)
  • ~ 1-2 tsp cajun seasoning
  • ~ 1 tsp chili powder
  • salt

Remoulade

  • ½ cup mayo
  • 2 tsp dijon
  • 1 tsp worchestire
  • 1 tsp tabasco
  • ½ tsp horseradish
  • 1 clove garlic
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • gherkins - ¼ cup chopped fine
  • 1 tsp lemon juice
  • a pinch of salt and pepper

Fixins’

  • lettuce
  • tomatoes
  • rolls

Start by preparing your shrimp. Make sure they are peeled, deveined, and rinsed. Then pat them dry and add the seasonings. You can go heavy on the cajun seasoning and light on the chili powder, unless you want to spice things up. It is such a simple seasoning, which such a huge flavor. I find it easier to skewer the shrimp that way they are easier to cook on the grill. If you are cooking them in a skillet, there is no need to skewer them. They will only take a few minutes on each side to cook. Note : this definitely varies and depends on the size you choose. They had very large shrimp on sale when I made these, so mine took about 5 minutes on each side until they were cooked through. The bigger the shrimp, the more texture and flavor you get.

For the sauce: mix together the ingredients and let the flavors come together.

Make sure you have good rolls for the sandwiches. Bread can make or break a sandwich! To assemble, cut the roll and scoop some of the bread filling out to make room. Add the shrimp, remoulade, lettuce, and tomato. These sandwiches are messy and amazingly delicious. Perfect for a summer night outdoors :)
