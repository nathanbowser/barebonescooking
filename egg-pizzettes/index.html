Egg Pizzettes

28 March 2015

You can have pizza for breakfast…if there’s an egg on it! Fact. I thought this would be a really fun recipe to share. Let’s be honest, this is not an everyday breakfast, but it is perfect for a lazy sunday morning or a special occasion breakfast. That is what we have here. Nate’s birthday breakfast! Believe it or not, I just used ingredients that I had on hand. That is the beauty of this. You can use any dough, any sauce, any cheese, and any veggies you have on hand. The possibilities are endless. Above is what I used for these babies.

  • ¼ red onion, sliced thin
  • ½ red pepper, sliced thin
  • fresh jalepeno, sliced
  • pizza sauce - or just canned tomato sauce
  • 1/2 cup ricotta cheese
  • pizza dough

Preheat your oven to 450 degrees.

Saute your veggies in olive oil with a pinch of salt and pepper. They don’t have to be fully cooked, just enough to lose their crunch.

Begin by rolling your pizza dough into small round circles. You want these to be small, individual sized. Spread each with a thin layer of sauce and ricotta cheese. Focus on the perimeter, leaving the middle empty so there is room for an egg. Next add veggies. Again, only around the outsides, leaving the center clear. Once ready, bake pizza for approximately 8 minutes. Remove pizza from oven, crack an egg in the middle of each and pop back in oven for approximately another 6 minutes. You want the egg to be set, but still have a runny yolk.

These are such a fun take on “breakfast pizza.”

Kristina Bowser
