Double Bean Burgers

13 May 2015

My husband and I can both agree - my black bean burger game has come a LONG way! I have been “attempting” to make non-mushy black bean burgers for years now, and I am happy to say that I have finally mastered it! I’m sure many of you have tried to make some form of bean burgers and they have not been the consistency you were hoping for. I have tried pan frying, baking, and even grilling. I would not try grilling, trust me. However, I have had great success in the combination of freezing the burgers, baking the burgers on high heat, then broiling them, which gives you a “burger-like” consistency and can stand up on a bun.

These are so great because they are an awesome meat free option, very filling, and you can play around with the ingredients and toppings to make what you like. I used a mixture of black beans and cannellini beans because I think the cannellini beans are so smooth and creamy that they balance out the black beans. You can add more or less spice with the jalapenos and chili powder. The toppings I used really compliment the black bean burger nicely, but the sky's the limit!

The chipotle mayo is so smoky and a bit spicy, the avocado is cool and creamy, and the sprouts are a fun and fresh twist. I do not usually use spouts in my cooking, but I love them when I eat them out. I saw them in my supermarket and scooped them right up for this recipe. I plan on using them top top sandwiches and wraps for the next week or 2.

Make these for your next meatless Monday or for your vegetarian friend!

Burgers

  • 2 cans black beans - rinsed and drained
  • 1 can cannellini beans - rinsed and drained
  • 1 large jalapeno, seeds removed
  • ½ red bell pepper
  • 2 cloves of garlic
  • 1 egg
  • 1 cup panko bread crumbs
  • handful cilantro
  • 1 1/2 tsp ground cumin
  • ½-1 tsp chili powder (if you like spice)
  • salt and pepper

Fixins'

  • hamburger buns
  • avocado
  • greens
  • red onion
  • chipotle mayo

Mayo

  • ⅓ cup mayo or light mayo
  • 2 tsp chipotles in adobo (I use the juice and not the actual peppers - can adjust to spice level)

First, give the peppers, garlic, and cilantro a rough dice. Toss them into the food processor and pulse until they are diced fine. Drain and rinse the beans and add them to a large bowl. Using your hands, get in there and “smoosh” the beans up. Of course you can use a fork if you’d like, but my grandmother always said - “a cook’s best tools are her hands.” She still cooks that way today. Add the food processor mix to the beans.

Next, add the seasoning and taste. This is your chance to taste before you add the egg. Add in the egg and mix to combine the mixture. Add panko bread crumbs and stir. These should soak up some of the excess liquid and make the mixture more dry.

At this point, I form my beans into burger patties on parchment paper or a silpat baking mat. Put the burgers into the freezer to firm up for about 1/2 hour - 1 hour. Preheat the oven to 400 degrees. You can take the burgers straight from the freezer to the oven. Bake the burgers for 20 minutes on 400 degrees then put them on the broil setting for 3-5 minutes to crisp up the tops.

For the mayo, just mix the 2 together and save for the topping.

To serve, add your burger to the bun, top with chipotle mayo, red onion, avocado, and alfalfa sprouts. I served mine alongside my mango slaw, which I will post later this week. Stay tuned...
