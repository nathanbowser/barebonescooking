Sun-dried Tomato Pasta Salad

05 July 2015

During the summer months, we all have so many occasions that could use a great side dish like this pasta salad. There are holidays like the 4th of July that just passed, BBQ’s, potlucks, and picnics. This is such a fun spin on pasta salad and it has a lighter feel. I usually use whole wheat pasta, which helps. I gave this salad a greek spin and used kalamata olives and feta cheese because they are salty and they compliment the sweet sun-dried tomato perfectly! The arugula has a nice sharp bite and also offsets any sweetness. All of these flavors go together so nicely and are truly the perfect compliment to any meal. It goes well with meat, fish, or can even be served alone as a light lunch.

  • 4 oz sun-dried tomatoes ~ 10 pieces packed in oil
  • ¼ cup of the oil from the sun-dried tomato jar
  • ½ cup olive oil
  • ¼ cup parmesan cheese
  • 1 cup arugula
  • ¼ cup kalamata olives - pitted and chopped
  • ⅓ cup feta crumbles
  • 1 lb pasta - I used whole wheat bowties

You can begin by cooking your pasta in a large pot of salted water. Cook to al dente and drain the pasta and let it cool just a little bit.

To make the “sauce”, begin by pulsing the sun-dried tomatoes in the food processor. I usually dump in about ¼ cup of the oil from the sun-dried tomato jar to thin it out a bit. Next, add an additional ½ cup of regular olive oil.

Once pasta has cooled, but is still a little warm, add the parmesan cheese and mix. Then add the sun-dried sauce you made. Allow this mixture to cool in the fridge until closer to the time you are ready to serve. This pasta salad is served cold or at room temperature. After the pasta salad has cooled, add in the arugula, feta crumbles, and kalamata olives. Mix and serve immediately or chill longer to let the flavors marry.

This salad is so perfect for BBQ’s or picnics because it can sit out and be served at any temperature. This recipe is a must before the end of the summer!

Kristina Bowser
