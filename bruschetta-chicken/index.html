Bruschetta Chicken

17 September 2015

It seems like everyone I talk to wants 2 things out of recipes: it has to be healthy and quick/easy. We all want to feel like we are eating a healthy meal, but we do not want to sacrifice on flavor or fullness. Also, most people are juggling work, family, and extra activities daily which can make it hard to put a delicious home cooked meal on the table. I think we can all relate. I am going to try my best to make more meals catering towards health and simplicity for everyone to enjoy. Starting with this chicken. This meal can come together in less than 30 minutes and it will not weigh you down. It is light, fresh, and delicious. This is a great weeknight dinner to make with the end of summer tomatoes before the fall produce rolls in.

Chicken

  • 1/3 cup brown sugar
  • 4 garlic cloves - minced or grated
  • 1/2 cup olive oil
  • 8 boneless, skinless chicken thighs

Bruschetta

  • 2 large ripe tomatoes - diced
  • 8 basil leaves - sliced into thin ribbons
  • 1 clove of garlic - grated
  • ~ ¼ cup olive oil
  • small mozzarella balls (optional)
  • salt and pepper

To marinate the chicken, mix the brown sugar, oil, and garlic in a shallow dish. Salt and pepper both sides of the chicken and add them to the marinade. This does not need to marinate long, but as with chicken, the longer the better.

You can grill the chicken or cook it on the stove in a very hot pan. Make sure the chicken is cooked all the way through, to 165 degrees.

While chicken is cooking, make the bruschetta. Just mix all the ingredients together and let the flavors come together. You can make this ahead of time and keep it chilled until you are ready to eat.

To serve : pile bruschetta high on top of chicken and enjoy!
