Summer Corn Salad

19 June 2015

This type of salad seems to be a staple in my family for most summer BBQ’s. Since summer corn has such a small window, we make the most of it as long as we can. When we enjoy corn on the cob, we usually leave the husk on the corn and grill it just like that. For this, I thought I would switch it up and boil the corn then use my indoor grill since the weather was bad. This makes it an easy option if you are already in the kitchen cooking. However, if you are already grilling, throw those babies on the grill and you’ll have the same outcome. The salad is light and refreshing for the summer weather and pairs nicely with most meat and seafood dishes.

  • 1 pint cherry tomatoes
  • 6 ears corn
  • 8 oz small mozzarella balls
  • 2 scallions- sliced thin
  • 1-2 tbsp honey
  • juice of 3-4 limes ~ 1/3 cup

Begin by slicing the mozzarella balls in half. If the cherry tomatoes are large, you can slice these in half too. Slice the scallions and set all of these in a large bowl. For the corn, you can grill it or boil it. I did both! A boil to cook it and a quick flip on the grill for color and some char.

I have a great trick for you to get the kernels off that cobb in the easiest and cleanest way possible. See my picture below : place a small bowl upside down in a large bowl, then place the corn cobb on the base of the small bowl and stead it. Using a sharp knife, shave the kernels off from top to bottom, so that they fall nicely in the bowl. So easy!

To make the dressing, it is based on your preference. It is just lime juice with honey, and salt and pepper. You can add more honey for sweetness or more lime for more tartness. Also, if you are making this ahead, you may want to make extra dressing since it will soak into the veggies. This is such a delicious, colorful side dish for any barbeque, picnic, or dinner this summer!

Kristina Bowser
