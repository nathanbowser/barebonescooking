Thai Tofu Lettuce Wraps

01 April 2015

Today I want to talk about something that maybe new and different to many of you...Tofu. Don’t be scared. When cooked correctly, it can be an awesome meatless option. I have been trying to make tofu delicious for quite some time now, and I have finally figured it out! I have tried many different ways: pan frying, baking, grilling, etc. The problem is that you want all the moisture out of your tofu and you want your final product to be crispy (and saucy). I break down my tofu preparation methods below, but after that, the possibilities are endless. Here, I decided to try a really fun take on serving the tofu, by putting it in lettuce wraps. These are bright, colorful, light, and absolutely delicious. I challenge you to try making tofu this way, especially if it is your first time. I think you will love it!

Wraps

  • 1 block tofu - extra firm
  • half a red bell pepper - sliced thin, 1 inch pieces
  • 1 carrot - shredded
  • 3 scallions - sliced
  • handful of cilantro - chopped
  • chopped peanuts or cashews
  • Crisp green lettuce leaves for the wraps - cabbage works best

Sauce

  • ⅓ cup creamy peanut butter
  • ⅓ cup low sodium soy sauce
  • 1 & ½ teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1 teaspoon sriracha - or more based on spice preference
  • 4 teaspoons honey - can add more depending on saltiness of soy sauce
  • 1 clove of garlic - grated

Tofu Prep: Most tofu is packed in water, and in order to achieve a desired crispiness, you must take the time to dry it completely. I usually start my tofu prep 1-2 hours before dinner. First, drain the block and wrap around a clean kitchen towel. Put a pan or something heavy on top and let sit for 30 minutes. Next, cut tofu into 8 equal rectangular logs and do the same - place in paper towel and let more moisture come out. Do this for as long as you can, but at least 20-30 minutes. At this point, preheat your oven to 400. Take each log, and cut into 8 equal cubes(about 1 inch each). Bake in the oven on a lined baking sheet for 25 minutes.

While the tofu bakes, prepare your sauce. Whisk ingredients together and let the flavors mingle. Sauce may be think, you can thin it out with 1-2 teaspoons of water, and whisk again to incorporate. Once the tofu comes out of the oven, throw into the sauce and let it absorb the delicious flavors for 15-20 minutes. At this point, you can saute your pepper and carrot shreds in about a teaspoon of oil. After the tofu is marinated, toss it in the pan with the veggies. Let it get thick and crispy for about 5 minutes. It may stick, let it, thats great flavor. At the end, toss in half the scallions and the cilantro.

To serve : Grab the outer pieces of the lettuce and lay on a plate. Fill each lettuce cup with as much filling as desired, a drizzle of more peanut sauce, more scallion, the peanuts, and sriracha (if you like spice!).

These are finger lickin’ good! You definitely won’t miss the meat.

Kristina Bowser
