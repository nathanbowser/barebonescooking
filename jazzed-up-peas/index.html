Jazzed Up Peas

06 April 2015

When I claimed a free ham from my grocery store for the holidays, we decided to have an impromptu Easter dinner. Unfortunately, I had to work Easter day. Lucky for me, my husband was in charge of the shopping, the ham, and all the prep! A 12 lb ham can be a bit much for just 2 people (can you say leftovers?), so we decided to invite the family over to join. I have had 2 recipe ideas for great sides to go with the ham bouncing around in my head for a week or so. I have always loved the classic combination of ham with peas and mashed potatoes. I decided to spruce up the peas and swap out the potatoes, and the results were fantastic! Stay tuned for the “mashed potato” swap...

  • 1 bag frozen peas (can use fresh when in season)
  • 5 slices bacon - chopped
  • half of an onion - chopped
  • approximately 3-4 teaspoons of heavy cream
  • handful of parmesan cheese (⅓ cup)

Begin by boiling the peas for about 5-7 minutes, then drain and rinse under cold water to stop the cooking process. Set aside and leave draining to remove most of the water.

Heat a cast iron or heavy bottomed skillet and add bacon once hot. Use a wooden spoon to break apart pieces and cook until crispy. Once crispy, remove bacon from pan and leave all the bacon grease. Throw in onions and saute for 5-7 minutes, until softened. Add in the peas and saute together for a few minutes, but not too long. You want the peas to stay crisp and not mushy. When I made these, I swirled 4 turns around the pan with the cream. Then add cheese and let the flavors meld. Add a little salt and pepper to taste at the end, but keep in mind the bacon and cheese are salty. That is it! This is a simple and really delicious way to jazz up your peas and is really nice along side a nice holiday ham.

Kristina Bowser
