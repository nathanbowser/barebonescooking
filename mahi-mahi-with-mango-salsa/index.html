Mahi-mahi with Mango Salsa

23 July 2015

Hey everyone! I wanted to post this fish for you all because it was a fairly new fish to me. It is definitely not a fish I can say I’ve ever cooked at home. I may have eaten it out before, but really was not sure of how it would taste prepared on the grill. Well, I can tell you this: it is a hearty, meaty, and delicious fish. It is substantial and very filling. It is mild and tastes fresh.

When I think of Mahi-Mahi I think of Hawaiian flavors. I thought the mango salsa would pair perfectly and it really did. It added a burst of bright flavor to each bite. The jalapeno spiced things up, the lime juice balanced out the sweetness of the mango and the red pepper was a nice crunch. This salsa would really pair well with any fish. Grilling the fish was a nice option because it infused some flavor into the fish and it cooked in no time. No pans to wash = happy Kristina. I hope you will all take my advice and try something new this summer!

Fish

  • 1 lb Mahi-Mahi
  • ~ 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • juice of ½ a lime
  • 3 garlic cloves - grated
  • ~ 1 teaspoon salt, and fresh cracked black pepper

Mango Salsa

  • 1 mango - diced
  • ½ red pepper - diced
  • ¼ cup red onion - diced fine
  • 1 jalapeno - diced fine
  • handful of cilantro - diced
  • juice of ½ a lime
  • a pinch of salt

Start by marinating the fish. Pat the fish dry, add the olive oil then salt and pepper and rub all over the fish. Then add the garlic and lime juice and let the fish marinate for half an hour.

Next, make your salsa. Once everything is diced, add it all to a bowl and then add a pinch of salt and the juice of half a lime. Taste for seasonings. The longer the salsa sits, the better.

Once the fish is ready, heat your grill to medium. Make sure to oil the grates before adding the fish so it doesn’t stick. Add the fish onto the grill, skin side down. Let the fish cook about 5 minutes. When you are ready to flip the fish, make sure it pulls away from the grill easily. If not, wait a little longer. The fish will tell you when it’s ready :) Cook the fish on the other side for 5-7 minutes until done. We used a thermometer to tell when it was finished cooking. The internal temperature should read 140 degrees. The fish should be firm to the touch and cooked through.

This is really easy for a weeknight meal, it comes together in a snap! We served the the fish on a bed of coconut rice with a side of grilled corn. Sweet summer meal.

Kristina Bowser
