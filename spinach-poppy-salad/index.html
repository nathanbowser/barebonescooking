Spinach Poppy Salad

19 May 2015

I want to start by saying that I am sorry for posting another salad recipe this soon, but I absolutely have to share this one! Plus, it’s spring-time and I think we all can use a little more salad in our lives. This dressing is a real winner. It is sweet, savory, and salty, with a nice bite from the onion. The ingredients go nicely together and really compliment and balance each other. This salad makes a great lunch or side for dinner. I think it would also be lovely at a BBQ as a light and fresh option.

As always, of course you can tweak the salad ingredients to use what you have and what you like. That is what I always do, and that is what inspired this salad! Hope you enjoy.

Salad

  • spinach leaves
  • 1 cup cooked whole wheat pasta (optional - I used leftovers)
  • sliced strawberries
  • ½ cup of crumbled feta - to taste
  • handful roasted sunflower seeds

Poppy Seed Dressing

  • ⅓ cup white vinegar
  • ½ cup olive oil
  • 2 tbsp white sugar
  • 2 tbsp grated white onion
  • 1 tsp poppy seeds
  • 1 heaping tbsp dijon mustard

Begin by making the dressing. Add the sugar to the vinegar and whisk until sugar is dissolved, about 2 minutes. Next, add grated onion - I use a regular box grater on the large holes to do this. This will give you the onion flavor without the sharp bite. Add the dijon mustard and then the olive oil and whisk until emulsified. Add the poppy seeds at the end.

Add the spinach and pasta to a large bowl and continue to add each layer on top. Mix with dressing and serve with extra sunflower seeds on top for a little added crunch!
