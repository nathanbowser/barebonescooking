Shrimp Quesadillas

30 June 2015

My apologies for posting another shrimp recipe so soon, but it is just such a great summer option! It is an easily-attainable, pretty affordable seafood option. It is nice to switch up classics with new flavors. That is just what I did here. Most people think of chicken, beef, or pork in their quesadillas, but swapping out meat for seafood is a great alternative. The shrimp is bold and spicy, the corn is sweet and fresh, and the scallions and beans are great additions. When pressed together with gooey cheddar cheese, each bite is bursting with great flavors! These are fun to serve cut up as an appetizer with a side of sour cream and tomatoes. You can also have them as a main meal with a vegetable or rice side, which is what we did. This is a great summer meal!

Quesadillas

  • 1/2 lb peeled and deveined shrimp
  • flour tortillas - 2 per quesadilla (this made 5)
  • chipotle in adobo, just the sauce
  • 1 corn cob - kernels removed
  • ½ can black beans
  • 1 scallion - sliced thin
  • cheddar cheese - grated

Toppings

  • Pico : diced tomato, diced red onion, diced jalapeno, cilantro
  • sour cream

I started this off by marinating my shrimp for about half an hour while I was preparing other things. I dried off my shrimp, added salt and pepper, and brushed them with chipotle sauce. I elf the actual peppers in the can and just used the sauce. That gives plenty of spice! They do not need long to marinate.

To make the filling for the quesadillas, start by cooking the shrimp. I made them on my indoor grill. You can grill them or pan fry them, whatever is easiest. If they are small like mine were, they only need a few minutes in each side. Once cooked, add them to a medium sized bowl. To that bowl, add you cooked corn kernels, black beans and scallions. This will be you filling.

If you have a panini or sandwich press, you can use this to press the quesadillas. If not, you can press them in a pan with a heavy pan on top. Add a tortilla to the bottom, add the filling leaving a small border around the edges and pile the cheese on top. Place another tortilla on top then close the lid and let it cook until the cheese is melted. Flip the tortilla carefully and let the other side cook until finished! You can serve these in a whole circle with a big dollop of sour cream and pico or you can cut them into wedges and top each wedge with the goodies. Delicious any which way you slice it!

Kristina Bowser
