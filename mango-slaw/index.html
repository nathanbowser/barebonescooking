Mango Slaw

14 May 2015

This is the slaw I served alongside the double bean burgers I posted earlier this week. It is so perfect for the grilling season and will be a great accompaniment to your BBQ’s. It comes together in no-time and all the ingredients are fresh. I have a feeling this will be on a regular rotation through the spring and summer cook-out season. It is crunchy, tangy, and sweet from the mango. What more could you ask for?

  • 1/4 cup greek yogurt
  • ¼ cup mayo
  • lime juice
  • 1 tsp dijon
  • 1 tsp sugar

  • *½ tsp celery seeds

  • ½ small green cabbage - shredded

  • ½ small red cabbage - shredded
  • 1 ripe mango - peeled and sliced thin
  • ½ red pepper - sliced thin

*I used ½ tsp of celery salt, so I omitted any extra salt. If you use plain celery seed, you may still need to add a pinch of salt.

First, make the dressing. Combine all the ingredients, mix, and taste for seasoning.

Chop and slice all your veggies and mango. Add in dressing and put it in the fridge so the flavors can meld. I did not use all the dressing. I would start with half, and then adjust from there. Sometimes, after it sits a while, it soaks up dressing and you need to add more later, so save some.

This is really a perfect side dish for any BBQ, burgers, grilled chicken, seafood, and everything! The best part is, it gets better the longer it sits, so it is a perfect make-ahead dish.

You’ll want to save this recipe for your upcoming summer BBQ’s!

Kristina Bowser
