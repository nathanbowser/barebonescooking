Cauliflower Mashers

07 April 2015

As promised, here is the other side dish to accompany the Easter ham. I know this has been a popular idea for some time now, but I think the roasted garlic in here really brings up the flavor. Lets face it, cauliflower can be boring. However, cauliflower can also be transformed to mimic other things with great results. Here, you can have the creamy and satisfying texture of mashed potatoes, but it is better for you! Don’t get me wrong - this still has a fair amount of butter, but this recipe makes enough for at least 6. So all in all, it is a better way to enjoy your favorite comfort food and get a little added health benefit. Win-Win.

  • 2 heads of cauliflower
  • 6 tablespoons of butter
  • ⅓ cup buttermilk
  • 1 head of roasted garlic (recipe below)

Roasted Garlic

Roasting garlic is a great way to add that delicious garlic flavor, without the serious bite. The roasting process mellows and sweetens the flavor of the garlic. It definitely has its place in cooking, and I think it fits perfectly in mashed potatoes and the like. You start with an entire clove (or 2) of garlic and you slice off the pointed tip, exposing just a tiny bit of each clove. Leave the root end intact. Flip garlic with the root end down and place on a piece of foil. Drizzle with olive oil and a pinch of salt and pepper and cover tightly. Place in the oven on 375 degrees for 50-60 minutes until fragrant and soft. Your house will smell amazing! Once finished, allow to cool. In order to get the cloves out, squeeze close to the root end and the cloves will just pop right out. You can even make this ahead or make multiple and keep in the fridge for other uses.

Cauliflower

Begin by cutting cauliflower off of the stem into smaller pieces. Set a large pot of water on the stove and bring to a boil. Boil the cauliflower until soft, the softer it is, the easier to mash. It took me about 15 minutes to boil. To mash the cauliflower, you can use an immersion blender or a food processor. If you do not have either of these kitchen gadgets, you can boil longer than 15 minutes and use a potato masher, but they will not be as smooth. Add in roasted garlic, butter, and buttermilk and mash together until desired creaminess. Add in a good amount of salt and fresh cracked black pepper. This is another extremely easy side dish and a really great way to “healthify” your meal. So go ahead, go for seconds!
